Eli Lilleskov is working with me in the summer of 2015 on a project to model the thermodynamic quantities of a strongly-coupled plasma using the AdS/CFT correspondence. This is accomplished by introducing a black hole into the AdS metric.

Eli’s research is supported by the Wallace Scholarly Activities Program. Eli will travel to Santa Fe to present a poster on his research at the 2015 Division of Nuclear Physics Meeting. The poster can be viewed here. He has also received a travel grant from the Conference Experience for Undergraduates.
