Associate Professor - Indiana State University

Sean Bartz

I study theoretical nuclear physics, with a focus on strongly interacting regimes where traditional techniques fall short. My work explores the quark-gluon plasma and holographic QCD models inspired by string theory, alongside computational approaches for extracting emergent physics.

Research Focus

Holographic QCD

Extra-dimensional models inspired by AdS/CFT to study meson spectra, phase transitions, and strongly coupled QCD.

Quark-Gluon Plasma

Modeling the physics of nuclear matter at extreme temperature and density, including meson melting and chiral behavior.

Computation and Pedagogy

Computational techniques for extracting observable predictions and context-rich problems that challenge student intuition.
