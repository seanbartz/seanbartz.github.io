Research Areas

Holographic nuclear theory

I study strongly coupled nuclear matter using holographic QCD (AdS/QCD) models inspired by the AdS/CFT correspondence. These extra-dimensional descriptions make otherwise intractable calculations more accessible and let us probe chiral symmetry breaking, meson spectra, and the quark-gluon plasma. My work develops soft-wall models, explores chiral phase transitions (including finite chemical potential), and investigates meson melting in thermal media.

Computational simulations of classical physics

I also work on computational and analytic models of classical systems, with an emphasis on collisions and rebound dynamics. These studies examine mass-spring-damper collision models, delayed rebounds in the two-ball bounce problem, and numerical simulations of magnet collisions, aiming to connect clean physical models with observable dynamics.
