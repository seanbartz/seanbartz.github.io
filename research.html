Journal Article Summaries (2018+)

These summaries highlight journal publications since 2018. A dedicated page is also available at.

Chiral phase transition at finite chemical potential in 2+1 flavor soft-wall anti-de Sitter space QCD (2018)

Authors: SP Bartz, T Jacobson

Venue: Physical Review C 97 (4), 044908

Explores the chiral phase transition at finite chemical potential using a 2+1 flavor soft-wall AdS/QCD model, highlighting how holographic ingredients shape the phase structure.

Dynamical AdS/Yang-Mills model (2018)

Authors: SP Bartz, A Dhumuntarao, JI Kapusta

Venue: Physical Review D 98 (2), 026019

Presents a dynamical AdS/Yang-Mills holographic model aimed at capturing strongly coupled QCD behavior beyond fixed-background approximations.

Numerical simulation of non-central collisions of spherical magnets (2022)

Authors: SP Bartz, J Shaw

Venue: European Journal of Physics 44 (1), 015003

Uses numerical simulations to study off-center collisions of spherical magnets, clarifying trajectory changes and collision outcomes.

Delayed rebounds in the two-ball bounce problem (2022)

Authors: SP Bartz

Venue: European Journal of Physics 43 (3), 035002

Analyzes delayed rebound behavior in the classic two-ball bounce problem, identifying when timing shifts lead to counterintuitive outcomes.

Gravity effects in mass-spring-damper models of inelastic collisions (2023)

Authors: SP Bartz

Venue: European Journal of Physics 44 (2), 025003

Studies how gravitational forces alter mass-spring-damper collision models, refining predictions for rebound and energy dissipation.

Chiral phase transition in soft-wall AdS/QCD with scalar-dilaton coupling (2024)

Authors: SP Bartz, RC Meadows, G Brock

Venue: Physical Review D 110 (2), 026027

Introduces scalar-dilaton coupling into a soft-wall AdS/QCD model and evaluates its impact on the chiral transition.

Chiral transition and meson melting within improved holographic soft wall models (2025)

Authors: A Ballon-Bayona, S Bartz, LAH Mamani, DM Rodrigues

Venue: Physical Review D 111 (2), 026011

Examines chiral transition behavior and meson melting in improved holographic soft-wall setups, comparing model variants.

N-body linear force law allowing analytic solutions (2025)

Authors: J West, SP Bartz

Venue: American Journal of Physics 93 (5), 376-381

Derives analytic solutions for N-body dynamics under a linear force law, offering a concise framework for classical mechanics applications.

The strong nuclear force binds together particles called quarks into hadrons, the larger particles that make up nuclear matter. At high energies the interactions between the quarks are weak, and there is a well-established technique known as perturbative quantum chromodynamics (QCD) that deals with such interactions. At lower energies the strength of the interaction grows, and perturbative techniques are no longer applicable.

One proposed model is the AdS/CFT (Anti-de Sitter space/Conformal Field Theory) correspondence, which relates certain strongly-coupled field theories to weakly-coupled gravitational theories with an extra dimension. Calculations that are analytically intractable in the field theory can be related to results from the gravity theory.
